No dyspnea:
  • No symptoms of shortness of breath
  • Shortness of breath only associated with coughing
Mild dyspnea:
  • Mild shortness of breath that does not interfere with daily activities (new mild shortness of breath with activities such as climbing one to two flights of stairs or walking briskly)
Moderate dyspnea:
  • Dyspnea that limits activities of daily living (difficulty walking up one flight of stairs, light housekeeping or meal preparation)
Severe dyspnea:
  • Dyspnea at rest
  • Patient unable to speak in complete sentences
  • Interferes with basic activities such as toileting and dressing
